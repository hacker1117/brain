# Pattern: Trust Before Transaction — Channel Relationships and Client Engagement Built on Credibility First

## Summary

Henry consistently delays or de-emphasizes direct sales when entering a new relationship — whether with a channel partner, an institutional client, or a new audience. His first engagement goal is to **demonstrate genuine capability and build credibility**, not to close deals. He explicitly rejects the 会销 (conversion event) model in favor of deliberate, low-pressure trust-building that positions future transactions as the natural downstream of a proven relationship.

This pattern operates at two levels simultaneously:
1. **Audience trust**: for the participants in a training or workshop, the goal is to be credible and useful, not to pitch
2. **Channel partner trust**: for the organization that hosts or co-delivers the event, the goal is to demonstrate AI and training capability so the relationship can yield future collaboration

## Observed Instances

### 1. 516 Salon planning with 魔方咨询 (2026-04-25)
[[wiki/personal/reflections/2026-04-25-516-salon-planning-wen-xinglai-a719b3]]

In the initial call with 魔方咨询 founder 温兴蕾, Henry and the salon are framed explicitly as non-commercial:

> 「沙龙目的：不是会销，而是公益性质的批量客户接触渠道，替代低效的一对一陌拜，目标每月一场」

The structure replaces cold outreach (一对一陌拜) with a recurring salon format. The commercial logic is deferred: the salon creates trust at scale, and transactions emerge from that trust downstream — not from the event itself.

温兴蕾's own observation that her HR clients are still 「浅浅的，停留在问答的阶段」 sets the bar: Henry needs to show a visibly higher level of AI application to establish credibility with this audience.

### 2. 516 Salon dual-layer goal structure (2026-05-14)
[[wiki/personal/reflections/2026-05-14-mofang-516-salon-dual-goals-1a7aea]]

Henry explicitly articulates a two-layer goal for the 516 salon that separates trust-building from transaction:

**Layer 1 (audience):**
> 「这场分享的核心目标还是先建立信任，至于后续跟不跟进都不太一定了。」

No push for conversion. The relationship outcome is held lightly. Trust is the deliverable, not a lead.

**Layer 2 (channel partner 魔方咨询):**
> 「这也是第一次和魔方咨询的合作，我们希望通过这次合作看到彼此的一些能力，尤其是给他展现出来我在 AI 方向上的能力和培训方向上的能力，这样以后也有机会以合作或者其他方式去给更多的企业进行服务」

The salon is simultaneously an audition: Henry is demonstrating his value to 魔方咨询 itself, so that the channel relationship deepens over time. The first collaboration is an experiment in mutual capability assessment, not a transaction.

### 3. GWC training: credibility through prior research, not prior materials (2026-05-14)
[[wiki/personal/reflections/2026-05-14-gwc-training-course-structure-planning-878953]]

The framing for the GWC AI training day is that the morning theory session should establish credibility with the audience before the afternoon hands-on. The design choice — keeping the morning from being heavily demo-focused — reflects the trust-first logic:

> 「不会花大力气在演示上」

Credibility is established through insight and calibration (knowing the audience's real situation from pre-training interviews), not through impressive demos. The demos come in the afternoon, after the audience has already formed a credibility judgment.

### 4. Soul Audit: client selection as a trust prerequisite (2026-05-12)
[[wiki/personal/reflections/2026-05-12-henry-soul-audit-self-disclosure-5b6bac]]

Henry's stated client selection criterion:
> 「我希望接的客户，是在增长的客户，或者在探索新的增长方向的客户」

And the negative case: he declines clients he describes as 「临终关怀式的效率优化」 — not because trust can't be built with declining businesses, but because trust that doesn't compound into a growing relationship is not the kind of trust this pattern targets. The trust-before-transaction pattern presupposes that the relationship is worth investing in — hence the growth filter.

## Pattern Structure

```
New channel relationship or client engagement
  → Do NOT: lead with pitch, product demo, or conversion ask
  → DO: establish credibility through demonstrated understanding
      - Show you know their reality (field research, pre-reading, calibrated framing)
      - Make your AI/expertise capability visible, not just claimed
      - Hold the commercial outcome lightly: "following up may or may not happen"

For channel partners specifically:
  → Treat first collaboration as mutual capability assessment
  → Your goal: make the partner see you as a resource they want to place with their clients
  → Partner's goal: see if your quality and fit justifies future co-delivery
  → Transaction logic: once trust is established, the channel generates referrals or co-engagements
    that Henry could not generate alone

For audiences:
  → Trust is the measurable deliverable of the session
  → Conversions, if they happen, happen downstream of trust — not during it
```

**What this avoids:**
- Conversion-pressure events (会销) that prime audiences to be defensive
- Cold outreach that has to rebuild credibility from zero each time
- Channel partners who place Henry with clients without understanding his capability level

**What it enables:**
- Scalable trust: one salon or training session reaches many people simultaneously
- Channel leverage: a trusted channel partner becomes a recurring referral engine
- Durable relationships: clients who chose to engage after seeing capability have higher loyalty
